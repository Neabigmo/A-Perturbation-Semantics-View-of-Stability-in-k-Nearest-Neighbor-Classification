\begin{abstract}
Leave-one-out evaluation is often interpreted as evidence that a classifier is
insensitive to individual training points.  We show that this interpretation
fails for deterministic $k$-nearest-neighbor classification, because LOO
deletion is tied to evaluation at the deleted point, whereas replacement
robustness concerns arbitrary replacement and arbitrary query evaluation.  For
every odd $k \ge 1$, and for even $k$ under deterministic tie-breaking, we
construct finite metric samples for which all LOO indicators vanish but a single
replace-one perturbation flips the prediction at a designated query.  The
separation is governed by a signed top-$k$ vote margin: LOO perturbations can
leave the margin on the safe side of the decision threshold, while a targeted
replacement crosses it.  Experiments and a margin-based diagnostic show that
this phenomenon is observable beyond hand-crafted witnesses.
\end{abstract}
